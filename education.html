Understanding Student Well being

Access professional insights to identify early indicators of mental health challenges and maintain academic balance.

Academic Burnout

A specific state of exhaustion caused by prolonged exposure to high pressure study environments.

  • Loss of motivation for core major subjects.
  • Feeling detached from student organizations or peers.
  • Noticeable decline in performance and focus.
  • Chronic physical fatigue despite adequate sleep.

Anxiety Disorders

Persistent and excessive worry that interferes with your ability to perform daily campus tasks.

  • Intense fear of public speaking or class presentations.
  • Over analyzing social interactions with professors or peers.
  • Physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat or trembling.
  • Difficulty concentrating on lectures due to racing thoughts.

Depressive Symptoms

More than just temporary sadness, these symptoms involve a long lasting low mood that affects functioning.

  • Persistent feelings of hopelessness regarding the future.
  • Significant changes in appetite or weight.
  • Loss of interest in hobbies previously enjoyed.
  • Withdrawing from social life and family obligations.

Social Isolation

Feeling disconnected or alienated from the LCCM community, which can trigger other mental health issues.

  • Intentional avoidance of common areas like the canteen.
  • Difficulty forming meaningful bonds within your block.
  • Feeling like you are an outsider in your chosen course.
  • Lack of a support system for academic challenges.
